The Media campaign

Grettings brothers and sisters!!

As previously mentioned, the settlers utilize their owned media outlets to disseminate propaganda and falsehoods, distorting reality and truth to manipulate public perception. Additionally, some settlers are active on social media, spreading the same propaganda. Their messages seem uniformly scripted, contributing to their broader agenda of dominating the native population.

Although some of us are actively speaking out online against the propaganda and falsehoods, our efforts seem to have limited impact compared to the extensive influence of the settlers. Many natives continue to accept and believe the settlers' narratives. Compounding this issue is our painful and complicated historyâ€”marked by enslavement, the loss of our identity, the imposition of foreign religions, and the portrayal of settlers as benevolent saviors. As a result, some natives are effectively working in the settlers' interest. To address this, we must launch a media campaign focused on countering the settlers' lies, propaganda, and distortions. Our goal is to educate and persuade the native population, shifting their support towards our movement and the cause of liberation.

Central to the media campaign will be the large-scale creation and dissemination of media content. This content will reveal the truth, expose falsehoods and propaganda, provide education, and serve as a reference, effectively countering the settler media's influence. The media will be distributed across various social media platforms and incorporated into multiple searchable websites, making it accessible through popular search engines for easy discovery by users.

Recently, the settlers launched a campaign promoting the false narrative of widespread white poverty in South Africa. Articles spreading this lie and propaganda appeared online, and many people believed it simply because it was available and unchallenged. Although some of us tried to refute these claims on social media, our efforts fell short of countering the extensive misinformation. Alarmingly, some natives were convinced by this falsehood, highlighting the severe risks of not having effective countermeasures against settler media and online propaganda.

The settlers have repeated this tactic with various issues, such as claiming there are no jobs for white people in South Africa and alleging a white genocide in the country, among other falsehoods.

Our media campaign will be a crucial tool in our struggle against the settlers and in mobilizing more supporters for our movement. Once the content is created and distributed, it will be essential for movement members to actively spread the truth using this content. For example, if propaganda about white poverty in South Africa appears on Twitter, thousands of us will respond by quoting the lies with accurate information and providing references. Similarly, if News24 publishes misleading claims about loans from MIF, we will counter with factual replies, presenting charts and evidence to expose the truth.

Another aspect of the campaign will involve targeting and exposing specific social media users or pages that disseminate propaganda. A coordinated effort by thousands of users, all presenting the same truth, could reduce the presence of these sources or at least alert natives to their unreliability.

Additionally, the media campaign will complement other initiatives. Given the volume of content that will be produced, there may be opportunities for temporary employment for students, allowing them to support themselves while gaining trust and confidence in the movement.
